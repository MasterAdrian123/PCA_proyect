NEWTON'S FIRST LAW

Newton's first law or law of inertia explains that every body tends to maintain its state of motion unless an external force modifies the system with zero net force

NEWTON'S SECOND LAW

Newton's second law states that the vector sum of all the forces acting on a body is equal to its mass multiplied by its acceleration

NEWTON'S THIRD LAW

When two particles interact, the force on one particle is equal and opposite to the force that interacts on the other particle. That is, if there is an external force, that force will be counteracted by another equal, but in the opposite direction.
